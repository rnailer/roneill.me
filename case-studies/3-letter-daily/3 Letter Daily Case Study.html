Origin

It started as a joke in a group chat

3 Letter Daily began in a UX-design group chat that had been quietly hijacked by Wordle since 2022, every morning a race to post the day's score before 9am. One Friday the bickering tipped over: someone called Wordle a guessing game, someone took it personally, and I fired off the message that started the whole thing, “I’m actually going to make a 3-letter Wordle.”

The thread that started it all, idea, pile-on, ship, rebuilt in the 3LD brand. Names lightly redacted.

From that message it was Friday night to Sunday night, one person, one weekend, about a hundred prompts and a concerning amount of coffee. By Sunday it was live: brand, daily-word logic, sign-in, streaks and share cards, the lot.

Problem

Three letters. Deceptively harder than five.

The premise reads simple, a Wordle you solve on a coffee break. But three letters is mechanically harder than five, not easier. Each guess returns less information, the structural constraints that make five-letter words guessable mostly vanish, and the space is packed with rhyming clusters, BAT, CAT, FAT, HAT, with no spare positions to throw away a probing guess.

How do you keep a daily puzzle fair inside a space this tight, without turning it into a coin-flip?

The difficulty isn't finding words, it's that a tiny space leaves no room to spend a guess gathering information. The fix lives in the answer pool, not the guess count.

Approach

Fix fairness at the source

Built with an AI-paired workflow, planning, building and interface each handled on a different surface, with handover docs holding context across sessions.

The full operating model is in the 333 Games studio piece. →

The bet

Curate the answer pool instead of padding the guess count, fix fairness at the source, and a three-letter puzzle stays solvable on a coffee break.

Build

A production stack, not a prototype

3 Letter Daily runs on the stack you'd reach for to ship something you mean to keep, nothing here is throwaway.

Stack Next.js 15 + TypeScript · Supabase / Postgres · Vercel · interface type in FT Baile

The one call worth flagging, guesses are graded server-side, so the day's answer never reaches the browser and can't be lifted from the page source. Auth is anonymous-first: you play from the first visit via a cookie, and only claim a streak (magic link or Google) if you want it across devices. The word list is generated by pipeline, with rhyming families pruned out of the answer pool.

Decisions

The non-obvious calls

01

Evaluate on the server

The answer is graded server-side and never shipped to the client, so it can't be lifted from the page source or network tab. Integrity before convenience.

02

Curate the pool, don't pad the guesses

Three-letter difficulty was solved by pruning rhyming-family answers, not by adding guesses, fairness fixed at the source.

Outcome

3 Letter Daily is live at 3letterdaily.com and soft-launched to friends and family, a real product, played daily, on a stack that can grow. Play numbers come next; the structural results are in.
